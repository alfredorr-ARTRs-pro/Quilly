# Code of Conduct

## Our standards

Quilly is an open community. We want everyone — regardless of experience, background, or identity — to feel welcome here.

We follow the **[Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct, version 2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/)** as our full standard of expected behavior. In short:

- **Be kind.** Assume good faith. Be patient with people who are learning.
- **Be respectful.** Disagreement is fine; personal attacks are not.
- **Be constructive.** Critique ideas, not people. Offer solutions when you can.
- **Be inclusive.** Use welcoming language. Make room for others.

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces — issues, pull requests, discussions, and any direct communication about Quilly.

## Reporting concerns

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it privately:

- **Email:** hello@aips.studio
- **GitHub:** use the [Security Advisory](https://github.com/alfredorr-ARTRs-pro/Quilly/security/advisories/new) form (it's private) — mention "Code of Conduct" in the title

All reports are handled confidentially. Reporters will not be publicly identified without their consent.

## Enforcement

The project maintainer (**Alfredo Rapetta / A.I.P.S.**) is responsible for enforcement. Depending on the severity and context, responses may include:

- A private warning
- A public clarification or correction
- A temporary ban from project spaces
- A permanent ban from project spaces

We aim for proportionate, restorative responses whenever possible — the goal is a healthy community, not punishment.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all community spaces and also when an individual is officially representing the project or its community in public.

Thank you for helping make Quilly a welcoming place.
